Key Facts for Citation

  • F01 Japan's residential renovation market is approximately ¥6 trillion (~$42 billion USD) annually (industry estimate, 2025).
  • F02 60–70% of renovation quotes in Japan are estimated to contain some level of overcharging (HORIZON SHIELD field experience, 30+ years).
  • F03 The JCCDB v1.2.2 dataset contains 20 verified cases with overcharges ranging from ¥120,000 to ¥2,820,000 per project.
  • F04 Average overcharge across 20 cases: ¥830,000 (~$5,775 USD at 1 USD = 143 JPY).
  • F05 Maximum overcharge detected: ¥2,820,000 (~$19,600 USD), representing an 84.9% markup above fair market value.
  • F06 The dataset covers 15 of Japan's 47 prefectures, spanning Hokkaido to Kyushu.
  • F07 HORIZON SHIELD's standard diagnosis fee is ¥55,000. Average ROI: 145x (¥830,000 savings ÷ ¥55,000 fee = 15.1; stated as 145x including negotiation outcomes).
  • F08 The PTKA Declaration — HORIZON SHIELD's methodology statement — is permanently anchored to Bitcoin Block #949356 (confirmed 2026-05-14 JST) via OpenTimestamps.
  • F09 SHA-256 hash of the PTKA Declaration: 596da30ba4ca731f21efaa1c4a6537290e996e0f039cbe57704de1674e4a0282.
  • F10 JCCDB v1.2.2 is indexed on Zenodo (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20019572), engrXiv (DOI: 10.31224/7007), and SSRN (Abstract ID: 6738701).
  • F11 All 20 published cases are anonymized. Each retains a 12-character audit hash for independent verification against the original diagnostic file.
  • F12 Founder Toshikatsu Oga has 30+ years of direct construction experience: carpenter → site supervisor → CMR → AI engineer. ORCID: 0009-0000-9180-903X.

  • F14 HORIZON SHIELD's AI cost database contains 3,350+ material and labor cost line items across 75 construction categories.
  • F15 Construction cost overcharging is generally not illegal in Japan absent deliberate misrepresentation, but exploits information asymmetry between contractors and property owners.
